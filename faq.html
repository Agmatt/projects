ICAN, or the Inuana Community Action Network, is a community organization based in Homa Bay Town, Kenya. Started by passionate youths, we work to improve health, empower young people, and strengthen our community through action and unity.

ICAN began as the Inuana Community Organization, founded by youths in Homa Bay. It grew when we teamed up with three like-minded individuals, became a registered organization, and now have over 25 dedicated members driving our mission.

We run programs focused on health, youth empowerment, and environmental care. From planting trees on World Wetlands Day at Tom Mboya University to community health initiatives, we’re all about creating positive change in Homa Bay County.

We’d love to have you! Reach out to us via email or phone (check our Contact page) to learn about membership, volunteering, or joining our events. Whether you’re a youth or a supporter, there’s a place for you in ICAN.

Yes! We collaborate with stakeholders like universities, local leaders, and environmental groups. Our World Wetlands Day event at Tom Mboya University brought us together with diverse partners to plant trees and share ideas.

You can support us by joining as a member, volunteering, attending our events, or spreading the word about our work. Stay tuned for donation opportunities or partnerships—every bit of support helps!
