Celebrating World Wetlands Day:

Tree Planting at Tom Mboya University

Inuana Community Action Network (ICAN) joined hands with students, faculty, and diverse stakeholders at Tom Mboya University in Homa Bay County, Kenya, to mark World Wetlands Day. This vibrant event, held on February 2, 2025, was a powerful testament to our shared commitment to preserving wetlands and fostering sustainable communities.

The day was filled with energy and purpose as ICAN members connected with environmental activists, local leaders, and university representatives. Together, we rolled up our sleeves and planted trees around the university grounds, contributing to the restoration of vital ecosystems. Wetlands, often called the "kidneys of the earth," play a critical role in water purification, flood control, and biodiversity. By planting trees, we aimed to enhance these natural systems, combat climate change, and create greener spaces for future generations.

The event was more than just planting trees—it was a celebration of collaboration. Conversations buzzed with ideas for sustainable practices, from wetland conservation to youth-led environmental initiatives. Stakeholders shared insights on the challenges facing wetlands in Homa Bay, sparking inspiration for ICAN’s ongoing mission to empower communities through health, youth engagement, and environmental action.

As the sun set over Tom Mboya University, the newly planted trees stood as symbols of hope and unity. ICAN is proud to have been part of this meaningful day, and we’re more determined than ever to continue our work in environmental advocacy and community empowerment. Join us in our journey to protect our planet and uplift Homa Bay—because together, we can make a lasting impact!
